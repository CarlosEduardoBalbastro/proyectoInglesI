The Legacy of Yerba Mate

A journey through time, from indigenous peoples to the present day, discovering the evolution of this iconic infusion.

A History of South America

A journey through time, from indigenous peoples to the present day, discovering the evolution of this iconic infusion.

Guarani origin

The First Creators

The consumption of yerba mate originated with the Guaraní people, who called it "caá" and considered it a gift from the gods. They used it as an energizing and medicinal drink, and as a central element in their rituals and trade.

Cultural Significance

Symbol of Connection

For the Guaraní, yerba mate was a symbol of friendship, hospitality, and community. Sharing the infusion strengthened social ties and was an act of respect among people.

The Arrival of the Jesuits

The First Cultivators

In the 17th century, Jesuit missionaries arrived in the region and observed the importance of yerba mate. They quickly recognized its economic potential and began perfecting its cultivation in their missions.

The "Jesuit Tea"

A Strategic Product

The Jesuits managed to domesticate the plant and established a large-scale production system. The infusion became known as "Jesuit tea" and became an important export product for the Spanish Crown.

The Expulsion of the Jesuits

A Period of Transition

With the expulsion of the Jesuits in 1767, the plantations were abandoned and production came to a standstill. The yerba mate industry entered a period of decline until new settlers and entrepreneurs resumed its cultivation.

Popularization

Mate in Society

Throughout the 18th and 19th centuries, mate consumption spread throughout South America, especially in the Río de la Plata region. The beverage became established as a social custom rooted in people's daily lives.

The Production Boom

The Role of Argentina

In the 20th century, Argentina became the world's leading producer and exporter of yerba mate. The industry developed significantly in provinces such as Misiones and Corrientes, boosting the local economy and consolidating its market position.

Mate Today

A Cultural Icon

Today, yerba mate is a fundamental element of the identity of Argentina, Uruguay, Paraguay, and southern Brazil. Its preparation and consumption ritual has been passed down from generation to generation, and today, its popularity extends throughout the rest of the world.

Benefits of Yerba Mate

More than a drink

In addition to its cultural value, yerba mate is valued for its health-promoting properties. It is a powerful antioxidant that helps prevent cellular aging and is a natural source of vitamins and minerals, which improve concentration and physical endurance.
